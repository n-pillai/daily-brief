01 World & Politics

Spain Closes Airspace to US Military Aircraft Over Iran War

BBC

Spain has closed its airspace to US planes involved in military operations against Iran, with Defense Minister Margarita Robles calling the war 'profoundly illegal and profoundly unjust.' The decision extends Spain's earlier ban on US use of jointly-operated military bases at Rota and Morón, marking Europe's strongest opposition to the US-Israel military campaign. Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez warned that responding to illegality with more illegality risks creating 'humanity's great disasters.'

Why it matters: This represents the strongest European challenge to US military operations in the Middle East and could significantly complicate American logistics for the ongoing Iran campaign.

US Fighter Jet Shot Down Over Iran as War Enters Fifth Week

Al Jazeera

A US military aircraft was shot down during operations over Iran, with one crew member rescued and search continuing for a second crew member according to US officials. Iran has also reported that a major bridge connecting Tehran and Karaj was destroyed in US-Israeli airstrikes, killing eight people and injuring 95 others. The incident occurred as the conflict continues into its fifth week, with Iran launching retaliatory missile and drone attacks across the Gulf region.

Why it matters: The loss of a US aircraft and continued infrastructure strikes signal an escalating conflict with no clear path to de-escalation despite international mediation efforts.

China and Pakistan Propose Five-Point Peace Plan for Iran War

Reuters · The Guardian

China and Pakistan jointly released a five-point peace initiative calling for an immediate ceasefire in the Gulf, swift launch of peace talks, and restoration of normal navigation through the Strait of Hormuz. The proposal came after Pakistani Foreign Minister Ishaq Dar met with Chinese Foreign Minister Wang Yi in Beijing, positioning Pakistan as a key mediator leveraging its ties to both the US and Iran. The plan calls for protection of civilians and civilian infrastructure, and a comprehensive peace framework based on UN Charter principles.

Why it matters: China's emergence as a potential mediator in a US-led conflict represents a significant geopolitical shift and could challenge American influence in resolving the crisis.
02 India

India Inducts Nuclear-Powered Submarine INS Aridhaman, Strengthening Naval Deterrence

Business Standard

India commissioned its indigenously-built nuclear-powered ballistic missile submarine INS Aridhaman on Friday, further strengthening the naval component of its nuclear triad. This is India's third submarine under the closely guarded SSBN program, following INS Arihant and INS Arighat. India joins an exclusive group of countries with nuclear-powered submarines including the US, Russia, UK, France and China.

Why it matters: This significantly enhances India's strategic nuclear deterrence capabilities and maritime security posture amid regional security challenges.

India Joins UK-Led Talks to Reopen Strait of Hormuz Amid Iran Crisis

Business Standard

Foreign Secretary Vikram Misri participated in a virtual meeting convened by the UK on Thursday to explore ways to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, a vital shipping route choked off by the US-Israeli war against Iran. More than 60 countries participated in the meeting where India emphasized the importance of navigation and unimpeded transit through international waterways. India is also supplying oil to Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, and Sri Lanka, with the Maldives seeking fuel supplies.

Why it matters: The closure of this critical waterway threatens global energy supplies and India's oil imports, while India positions itself as a regional energy supplier to neighboring countries.

India-UK Free Trade Agreement Expected to Come into Force Within 30-45 Days

Business Standard

Commerce and Industry Minister Piyush Goyal announced that the India-UK free trade agreement, signed in July 2025, is likely to be implemented by May 1, 2026. The agreement has already passed both Houses of Parliament in both countries. Goyal held a bilateral meeting with UK's Secretary of State for Business and Trade Peter Kyle on the sidelines of the 14th WTO ministerial conference in Cameroon.

Why it matters: The implementation will deepen economic ties between two major economies and provide Indian businesses with enhanced access to UK markets.

New Tax Rules on Foreign Income and Remittances Take Effect

Business Standard

The Central Board of Direct Taxes has notified new Income-tax Rules, 2026 under the Income-tax Act, 2025, which came into force on April 1. The new rules significantly tighten reporting requirements for foreign transactions, with increased focus on uniformity, transparency and data consistency. Non-resident taxpayers face higher compliance burden, especially for tax treaty claims, foreign remittances and foreign tax credit.

Why it matters: These changes will impact Indians working abroad, receiving foreign remittances, or conducting international business transactions.

04 Business & Finance

Dated Brent Oil Price Surges Above $140, Highest Level Since 2008

Bloomberg

Dated Brent, the price for real-world oil shipments in the North Sea, reached $141.37 on April 2, marking the highest level since 2008 and surpassing levels seen during Russia's Ukraine invasion. The surge reflects the severe disruption to global oil supply caused by Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz during the ongoing U.S.-Iran conflict. Analysts warn the futures price is not accurately reflecting the true tightness in physical oil markets.

Why it matters: This represents the worst energy crisis in history according to the IEA, with 12 million barrels per day lost—more than double the 1970s oil crises—threatening global recession.

US Payrolls Rose by 178,000 in March, More Than Expected

CNBC · Wall Street Journal

The U.S. economy added 178,000 jobs in March, significantly exceeding the expected 57,000 and rebounding from February's revised loss of 133,000 jobs. Healthcare led gains with 76,000 jobs added, including 35,000 workers returning from a Kaiser Permanente strike. However, wage growth slowed to 3.5% annually—the lowest since May 2021—and the labor force contracted by 396,000. Unemployment held at 4.3%.

Why it matters: The mixed report—stronger job growth but weaker wage gains and shrinking labor force—keeps the Federal Reserve on hold as the Iran war drives energy inflation.

US Oil Prices Soar 11% as Trump's Iran War Speech Stokes Escalation Fears

CNBC

U.S. West Texas Intermediate crude futures surged more than 11% to close at $111.54 per barrel on April 2, while Brent crude gained nearly 8% to settle at $109.03. The spike followed President Trump's Wednesday evening national address warning of further military aggression against Iran in the next two to three weeks, dampening hopes for imminent de-escalation. The Strait of Hormuz remains largely closed, blocking approximately 20% of global oil flows.

Why it matters: The continued closure of the world's most critical oil chokepoint threatens to push energy prices even higher and deepen global economic disruption.

Iranian Oil Trades at Premium to Brent for First Time Since 2022

Bloomberg

Iran's main oil export grade swung to a premium of $1 per barrel over global Brent crude on March 26, marking the first time since May 2022 that Iranian oil has traded above the benchmark. The reversal from typical steep discounts came just days after the U.S. temporarily rolled back some sanctions on Iranian oil. This unusual pricing dynamic reflects the extreme market distortions caused by the conflict and sanctions shifts.

Why it matters: The premium pricing for Iranian oil highlights the severity of global supply disruptions and the complex sanctions dynamics during wartime.

Why it matters: This ruling could fundamentally weaken states' authority to regulate medical practice and protect patients from harmful or discredited treatments.

Social Sciences Research Shows Low Replication Rates

Science

A large-scale assessment found that while over 85% of 110 articles from leading economics and political science journals were computationally reproducible, about half of research in the social sciences doesn't replicate. About 25% of evaluated articles contained non-trivial coding errors, and 72% of statistically significant results remained significant in robustness checks.

Why it matters: This assessment provides crucial insights into the reliability of social science research and highlights systemic issues in research reproducibility that affect policy decisions.
