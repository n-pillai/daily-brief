02 India

India Invokes Emergency Powers to Secure Cooking Gas Supply as Middle East Conflict Disrupts Imports

Business Standard

The Government of India invoked the Essential Commodities Act to regulate LPG and natural gas supply, issuing the Natural Gas Supply Regulation Order 2026 that prioritizes allocation to domestic PNG, CNG, and fertilizer plants. The Ministry of Petroleum ordered refineries to maximize LPG production as 13 tankers carrying 3.5 lakh tonnes of LPG head from the U.S. to India. The emergency measure affects 33 crore households dependent on cooking gas, with 85-90% of India's LPG imports typically passing through the now-disrupted Strait of Hormuz.

Why it matters: This exposes critical vulnerabilities in India's energy security and marks the first use of emergency commodity controls since the pandemic.
