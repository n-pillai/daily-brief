Indian Rupee hits record low of 95.22 despite RBI's currency market intervention

Business Standard

The rupee touched an all-time intra-day low of 95.22 against the US dollar on March 30, erasing early gains from the RBI's dramatic intervention to cap bank currency positions at $100 million. Oil prices surging to $120 per barrel due to the Iran-US war, foreign portfolio outflows of over $13 billion in March, and the RBI holding over $100 billion in short positions have driven the currency's sharp decline. India's forex reserves fell $30 billion in March as the central bank sold dollars to support the rupee.

Why it matters: The rupee's historic collapse threatens to worsen inflation through higher import costs, particularly for oil-dependent India, while depleting foreign reserves limits the RBI's ability to stabilize the currency further.
