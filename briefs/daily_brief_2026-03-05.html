Kraken Becomes First Crypto Firm to Gain Federal Reserve Master Account

Wall Street Journal

Cryptocurrency exchange Kraken's banking unit, Kraken Financial, has secured approval for a Federal Reserve master account, granting it direct access to the Fed's core payment systems including Fedwire. This makes Kraken the first digital asset bank in U.S. history to receive such approval, allowing it to move money on the same rails used by traditional banks and credit unions. The approval is limited and does not include the full suite of services available to traditional banks, such as earning interest on reserves.

Why it matters: This milestone marks a historic convergence of crypto infrastructure and traditional banking, potentially opening the door for broader institutional adoption of digital assets within the U.S. financial system.
