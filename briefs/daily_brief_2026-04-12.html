Experiments Ring the 'Death Knell' for Sterile Neutrinos

Quanta Magazine

New experimental results published on April 8 have effectively ruled out the existence of sterile neutrinos, hypothetical particles that some physicists hoped could explain dark matter and other mysteries of the universe. Multiple detector experiments found no evidence for the additional neutrino type beyond the three known flavors, closing the door on one of the more popular extensions to the Standard Model of particle physics.

Why it matters: The findings force physicists to look elsewhere for explanations of dark matter and neutrino anomalies, reshaping the search for physics beyond the Standard Model.
