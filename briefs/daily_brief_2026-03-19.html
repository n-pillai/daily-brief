02 India

HDFC Bank chairman resigns over ethics concerns, shares plunge 8% to 52-week low

Business Standard

Atanu Chakraborty resigned as part-time chairman of HDFC Bank on March 18, citing that certain practices within the bank over the past two years were 'not in congruence with my personal values and ethics.' The resignation sent HDFC Bank shares to a 52-week low, falling over 8% and wiping out Rs 1.03 lakh crore in investor wealth. The Reserve Bank of India approved Keki Mistry as interim chairman for three months, and stated there are no material concerns regarding the bank's governance or conduct.

Why it matters: As India's largest private sector lender and a systemically important financial institution, governance concerns at HDFC Bank raise questions about board-management alignment and could impact investor confidence in the country's banking sector.
