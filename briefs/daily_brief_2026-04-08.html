Iran and US Agree to Two-Week Ceasefire, Strait of Hormuz to Reopen

Al Jazeera

Iran accepted a two-week ceasefire following President Trump's threats to destroy civilian infrastructure if the Strait of Hormuz was not reopened by his 8pm Tuesday deadline. Iran's Foreign Minister confirmed safe passage through the key waterway will be permitted for two weeks under coordination with Iran's armed forces, with negotiations to begin Friday in Islamabad, Pakistan. The agreement came just 90 minutes before Trump's deadline would have expired.

Why it matters: This temporary ceasefire prevents immediate escalation in a six-week war that has disrupted global energy supplies and threatened widespread civilian casualties from potential attacks on Iranian infrastructure.

Vice President Vance Campaigns for Hungary's Orban Ahead of Critical Election

Washington Post

US Vice President JD Vance traveled to Budapest to support Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orban's reelection campaign ahead of the April 12 election, meeting with Orban and appearing at a campaign rally. Orban, who has maintained close ties to Russia and called the EU a greater threat to Hungary than Russia, is trailing in polls against the pro-European Tisza party led by Péter Magyar. The unprecedented intervention by a sitting US Vice President in a European ally's election has raised concerns about American democratic norms.

Why it matters: The visit represents a stark shift in US foreign policy toward embracing illiberal democracies and could reshape transatlantic relations if Orban wins reelection with American backing.

Australia's Most Decorated Veteran Ben Roberts-Smith Charged with War Crimes

Associated Press

Ben Roberts-Smith, a 47-year-old former Special Air Service Regiment corporal awarded the Victoria Cross and Medal of Gallantry, faces charges for allegedly killing five unarmed Afghans while serving in Afghanistan from 2009 to 2012. He appeared in court briefly and was remanded in custody until June 4, becoming only the second Australian veteran of the Afghanistan campaign to be charged with a war crime. The charges follow a years-long investigation into alleged atrocities by Australian special forces.

Why it matters: The arrest of Australia's most decorated living soldier marks a significant moment of accountability for alleged wartime atrocities and sends a message about military conduct standards.

Artemis II Astronauts Complete Historic Moon Flyby, Break Apollo 13 Distance Record

Associated Press

NASA's Artemis II crew completed a record-breaking lunar flyby mission, surpassing Apollo 13's distance record as they looped approximately 4,000 miles from the lunar surface. The successful mission represents a major milestone in NASA's plan to return humans to the lunar surface.

Why it matters: This mission validates critical systems and procedures for future lunar landings and demonstrates renewed momentum in human space exploration after decades of near-Earth orbit operations.
