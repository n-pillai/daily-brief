Presolar Grains Older Than the Sun Reveal Formation of Solar System

Quanta Magazine

Scientists analyzing presolar grains—crystals that formed before our solar system existed—have uncovered new details about the stellar processes and supernova events that seeded the molecular cloud from which the Sun and planets formed 4.6 billion years ago. The isotopic signatures in these ancient grains provide a chemical record of stellar evolution and nucleosynthesis in previous generations of stars.

Why it matters: Understanding the material heritage of our solar system helps explain the chemical composition of Earth and other planets, including the elements essential for life.
