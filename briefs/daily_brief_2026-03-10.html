Oil Prices Whipsaw as Trump Signals Possible End to Iran War, Hints at Seizing Strait of Hormuz

CNBC

Oil prices experienced extreme volatility with Brent crude briefly topping $119 per barrel before falling back near $90 after President Trump suggested the Iran war could soon end and hinted at seizing control of the Strait of Hormuz. The strategically vital waterway, through which 20% of global oil supplies normally pass, has been effectively closed since U.S.-Israeli strikes began, forcing major producers including Saudi Arabia and Iraq to cut production. U.S. gasoline prices have surged 16% in a week to $3.48 per gallon.

Why it matters: The closure of the Strait of Hormuz represents an unprecedented energy crisis threatening global supply chains and could force central banks to delay rate cuts despite economic weakness.

India Cabinet Approves Easing Investment Restrictions on China to Boost Capital Inflows

Reuters

India's cabinet on Tuesday approved easing restrictions on foreign investments from China and other land-bordering nations to boost capital inflows in the world's fifth-largest economy. India tightened scrutiny of investments from Chinese companies in 2020 after border clashes, requiring security clearance from home and foreign ministries. Facing steep U.S. tariffs, New Delhi has cautiously begun to revive ties with Beijing since last year, with PM Modi visiting China in August and both countries resuming direct flights and easing visa procedures.

Why it matters: This marks a significant shift in India-China economic relations after years of tension following the 2020 border clashes, potentially opening up billions in new investment amid global trade realignment.
