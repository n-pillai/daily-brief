Hungary's Viktor Orban Ousted After 16 Years as Opposition Wins Landslide Election

Al Jazeera

Hungarian Prime Minister Viktor Orban conceded defeat after Peter Magyar's Tisza party won a two-thirds majority with 53.6 percent of the vote in parliamentary elections. Magyar's center-right party secured 138 seats in the 199-seat parliament, ending Orban's 16-year rule with record turnout of nearly 80 percent.

Why it matters: Orban's defeat removes a key ally of President Trump and Putin from power in Europe and could reshape Hungary's relationship with the EU and its role in blocking aid to Ukraine.

China Announces New Ties with Taiwan After Xi Jinping Meets Opposition Leader

Washington Post

China announced it will resume direct flights to Taiwan and lift bans on Taiwanese aquaculture imports following a meeting between President Xi Jinping and Kuomintang opposition leader Cheng Li-wun. Xi told Cheng that China will 'absolutely not tolerate' Taiwan independence in their first such meeting in a decade. Taiwan's government criticized the meeting as undermining sovereignty and facilitating Beijing's unification agenda.

Why it matters: The meeting represents Beijing's strategy to engage Taiwan's opposition while the ruling party resists closer ties, potentially reshaping cross-strait relations ahead of Taiwan's next election cycle.
