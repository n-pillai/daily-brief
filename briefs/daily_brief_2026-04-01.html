Global Visa Rules Tighten Across US, UK, Canada and EU

Additional: Business Standard

Multiple countries implemented stricter immigration rules effective April 1. The U.S. mandated updated H-1B Form I-129 with detailed wage disclosures and enhanced green card screening. UK visa fees increased across multiple categories, while the EU began implementing biometric border checks. The changes increase costs and processing times for international students, professionals, and families seeking work or study opportunities abroad.

Why it matters: These coordinated policy shifts reflect a global trend toward stricter migration controls that will reshape international mobility patterns and labor markets.
02 India

India Launches Digital Census 2027 with First-Ever Caste Enumeration

Business Standard

India's 16th Census and eighth since Independence began April 1, marking the first time the exercise will be conducted digitally with self-enumeration options. The census will be conducted in two phases: House Listing and Housing Census from April to September 2026, followed by Population Enumeration in February 2027. For the first time, caste enumeration will also be conducted during the second phase, a politically significant decision that addresses long-standing demands from various communities.

Why it matters: This is India's largest census exercise globally and introduces transformative digital methods while adding caste enumeration, which will shape policy-making, welfare programs, and political discourse for decades.

India Bans Chinese CCTV Cameras Over Security Concerns

Business Standard

India restricted Chinese-origin CCTV cameras from companies like Hikvision and Dahua starting April 1 due to national security concerns and new certification requirements. Chinese vendors previously held about one-third of India's $5-7.5 billion video surveillance market, but Indian manufacturers now control over 80% as of February 2026. The move may cause temporary supply shortages and price increases but creates opportunities for domestic manufacturers.

Why it matters: The ban accelerates India's push for technology self-reliance while addressing cybersecurity vulnerabilities in critical surveillance infrastructure.

Securities Transaction Tax Hike on Futures and Options Trading Takes Effect

Additional: Business Today

As outlined in Union Budget 2026-27, the Securities Transaction Tax on futures and options trading increased from April 1. STT on futures contracts rose from 0.02% to 0.05%, while options premiums increased from 0.10% to 0.15%. The government also implemented new corporate action rules, including taxing share buybacks under capital gains. These changes specifically target derivative traders while leaving other market segments untouched.

Why it matters: The tax hikes aim to cool speculative trading in derivatives markets while boosting government revenue, potentially reshaping retail trading behavior.

New Income Tax Act 2025 Introduces Structural Reforms

Additional: Business Today

The new Income Tax Act 2025 took effect April 1, 2026, introducing structural reforms beyond immediate tax relief. Key changes include a unified 'Tax Year' replacing the Financial Year-Assessment Year system, stricter HRA documentation requirements with mandatory PAN disclosure, and new Form 130 replacing Form 16. The focus shifts from tax savings to transparency, compliance, and improved reporting mechanisms.

Why it matters: The comprehensive overhaul represents the first major rewrite of India's tax code in decades, potentially improving compliance but requiring significant adjustment from taxpayers and businesses.
