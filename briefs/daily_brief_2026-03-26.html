02 India

14 Killed, 23 Injured as Bus Collides with Truck and Catches Fire in Andhra Pradesh

Business Standard

At least 14 people were charred to death and 23 others injured after a private bus traveling from Telangana to Nellore district collided with a gravel-loaded tipper truck near Rayavaram in Markapuram district, Andhra Pradesh, on Thursday morning. The collision triggered a massive fire that engulfed both vehicles, trapping passengers in the rear of the bus. Chief Minister N Chandrababu Naidu ordered a comprehensive inquiry and Prime Minister Modi announced ex-gratia of Rs 2 lakh to families of the deceased.

Why it matters: This deadly accident highlights persistent road safety concerns on Indian highways, particularly involving private buses and heavy vehicles at sharp turns and quarry zones.

Lok Sabha Passes Finance Bill 2026 with Major Relief for Startups and Businesses

Business Standard

The Lok Sabha passed the Finance Bill 2026 with amendments offering significant relief to businesses and startups. Key changes include limiting new buyback taxes only to transactions under Section 68 of the Companies Act 2013, raising the turnover threshold for startup tax holidays from Rs 100 crore to Rs 200 crore (and Rs 300 crore for deep-tech startups), and removing arrest and detention as tools for recovering tax arrears. The bill also introduces a mandatory 30-day minimum period for taxpayers to respond to reassessment notices.

Why it matters: These amendments represent a major shift toward a more business-friendly regulatory environment, reducing compliance burden and fear of criminalization while encouraging startup growth and innovation in India's tech ecosystem.

India Maintains WTO Opposition Despite Supporting Investment Facilitation

Business Standard

India stated it supports initiatives to facilitate investment flows to developing and least developed countries, but maintained that the World Trade Organization is not the appropriate forum for such matters. This position was reiterated ahead of the 14th WTO ministerial meeting (MC14) scheduled for March 26-29 in Yaoundé, Cameroon. India has not joined the China-led Investment Facilitation for Development (IFD) agreement backed by 128 WTO members, arguing it would dilute the multilateral nature of the organization.

Why it matters: India's stance highlights growing divisions within the WTO over its mandate and reflects concerns about maintaining developing country negotiating power in global trade governance.
