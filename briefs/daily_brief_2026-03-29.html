01 World & Politics

Foreign Ministers from Pakistan, Turkey, Egypt and Saudi Arabia Meet in Islamabad to De-escalate US-Israel War on Iran

Al Jazeera

Foreign ministers from four major Muslim nations convened in Islamabad for diplomatic talks aimed at de-escalating the month-long US-Israeli military campaign against Iran. The meeting comes as Pakistan has been mediating indirect negotiations between Washington and Tehran, with the US sharing a 15-point peace plan through Pakistani channels. Iran's parliament speaker warned the US was plotting a ground attack despite public diplomatic engagement.

Why it matters: This represents the most significant multilateral diplomatic effort to end a conflict that has killed nearly 2,000 people in Iran, over 1,100 in Lebanon, and disrupted global oil supplies through the Strait of Hormuz.

Lebanon Expels Iranian Ambassador Over Hezbollah Support, Gives Envoy Until March 29 to Leave

Al Jazeera

Lebanon's foreign ministry declared Iranian ambassador Mohammad Reza Raouf Sheibani persona non grata due to Iran's financial and military support of Hezbollah, ordering him to leave by March 29. The ministry clarified the expulsion doesn't sever diplomatic relations between the two countries. The move comes amid escalating conflict that has killed over 1,100 people in Lebanon since early March.

Why it matters: The expulsion signals Lebanon's attempt to distance itself from Iran amid mounting international pressure and domestic casualties from the ongoing regional conflict.

Russia Vows 'Asymmetric Response' to UK Detention of Shadow Fleet Vessels

Reuters

Russia condemned the United Kingdom's decision to board and detain shadow fleet vessels as hostile, vowing a 'political, legal and asymmetric' response to protect Russian interests. British Chief of the General Staff Roland Walker warned the UK is on an 'inevitable collision course' with Russia. The confrontation occurs as both nations face competing priorities with ongoing conflicts in Ukraine and Iran respectively.

Why it matters: The escalating UK-Russia maritime dispute threatens to open a new front in Western-Russian tensions while both are engaged in separate major conflicts.

Denmark's Social Democrats Win Election But Record Worst Result Since 1903, Coalition Talks Begin

The Guardian

Prime Minister Mette Frederiksen's Social Democrats emerged as the largest party with 21.9% of votes in the March 24 election but suffered their worst performance in over a century. Neither left nor right blocs secured a parliamentary majority, leaving centrist Foreign Minister Lars Løkke Rasmussen as kingmaker. The election followed tensions over US President Trump's threats regarding Greenland, though voters focused primarily on economic issues.

Why it matters: The fragmented result could lead to a prolonged period of political uncertainty in Denmark at a time of heightened geopolitical tensions in the Arctic and European security concerns.

Italy Rejects Judicial Reform in Referendum, Dealing Blow to Prime Minister Meloni

Reuters

Italian voters rejected a constitutional amendment to restructure the judiciary by 54% to 46% in a referendum on March 23, marking a significant defeat for Prime Minister Giorgia Meloni's government. The reform would have separated career paths for judges and prosecutors and introduced selection by lottery for judicial governing bodies. Meloni conceded defeat but said she would not resign, though the result is seen as boosting opposition parties ahead of 2027 elections.

Why it matters: The referendum defeat weakens Meloni's political position and may embolden opposition forces in Italy's fractious political landscape ahead of national elections.
02 India

WTO Talks Near Deal on Reform Roadmap Amid U.S.-India E-Commerce Deadlock

Reuters

Trade ministers at a WTO meeting in Cameroon are close to agreeing on a reform plan for the organization, though wrangling continues over extending the moratorium on customs duties for electronic transmissions. The talks include efforts to bridge differences between the U.S. and India over the e-commerce moratorium, which expires this month and is seen as a test for the WTO's relevance following tariff-fueled trade turmoil and Iran war disruptions.

Why it matters: The outcome will determine whether the WTO can remain relevant in regulating global digital trade worth trillions of dollars, at a time when multilateral institutions face mounting challenges.

Markets Now See Fed's Next Move as Potential Rate Hike as Oil Prices and Inflation Fears Rise

CNBC

Traders in the futures market pushed the probability of a Federal Reserve rate increase by the end of 2026 to 52%, the first time it has crossed the 50% threshold according to CME Group. The shift comes as global benchmark crude prices topped $110 per barrel, combining with other developments signaling mounting inflation pressures as the Iran war continues.

Why it matters: This represents a dramatic reversal in Fed policy expectations, from rate cuts to potential hikes, driven by energy-fueled inflation concerns that could reshape investment strategies across all asset classes.

Oil Prices Surge Past $110 as Iran War Escalates, Stocks Enter Correction Territory

CNBC

Brent crude futures jumped to $108.01 per barrel while markets whipsawed on geopolitical signals from the Middle East conflict. The Nasdaq Composite closed in correction territory, down more than 10% from its high, while the Dow tumbled nearly 800 points. President Trump expressed confidence that oil prices will reverse, telling Treasury Secretary Bessent that prices 'have not gone up as much as I thought' and will likely come back down.

Why it matters: The combination of surging energy prices and stock market correction signals growing investor anxiety about stagflation—the toxic mix of slowing growth and rising inflation.

Import Prices Jump 1.3% in February, Biggest Monthly Gain in Nearly Four Years

CNBC

The Bureau of Labor Statistics reported import prices rose 1.3% in February, exceeding the 0.6% forecast and marking the largest monthly increase in nearly four years. Export prices jumped 1.5%, well ahead of January's 0.6% increase. The notable increase in nonfuel goods costs suggested underlying inflationary pressures were building even before the recent energy spike, pointing to pipeline inflation ahead.

Why it matters: The data suggests inflation was accelerating before the Iran conflict drove oil prices higher, complicating the Federal Reserve's path forward on monetary policy.

OECD Forecasts U.S. Inflation at 4.2% for 2026, Well Above Fed Projections

CNBC

The Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development forecast all-items U.S. inflation at 4.2% for 2026, a sharp increase from its prior projection of 2.8% and significantly higher than the 2.7% Fed officials estimated in their recent forecasts. The Iran war and its impact on global energy markets will keep headline inflation well above the Federal Reserve's projections, the international policy group warned.

Why it matters: The sharp upward revision in inflation forecasts from a respected international body adds pressure on the Federal Reserve to consider tightening monetary policy despite economic growth concerns.
