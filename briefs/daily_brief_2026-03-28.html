Iran Grants India 'Friendly Nation' Status for Hormuz Passage Amid War

The Hindu

Iran's Foreign Minister declared five nations—India, China, Russia, Iraq, and Pakistan—permitted to move ships through the Strait of Hormuz, which Iran claims as territorial waters. Iran thanked India for helping rescue Iranian ships after the US Navy sank the Iranian frigate IRIS Dena. At least four India-flagged vessels have recently transited the strait as traffic drops to single digits.

Why it matters: This strategic exemption secures India's critical oil supply route as the conflict threatens to disrupt 30% of global oil shipments through the strait.

02 India

Government Slashes Fuel Excise Duty by ₹10 per Litre to Shield Consumers

Business Standard

The Indian government cut central excise duty on petrol and diesel by ₹10 per litre each, bringing petrol duty to ₹3/litre and diesel to zero. The emergency decision comes as international crude prices surged from $70 to around $122 per barrel following the US-Israel strikes against Iran. Oil marketing companies are currently facing losses of around ₹24 per litre on petrol and ₹30 per litre on diesel despite the duty cut.

Why it matters: This represents a major fiscal sacrifice by the government to prevent inflationary pressures during a critical geopolitical crisis, though it will significantly impact tax revenues.

PM Modi to Inaugurate ₹11,200 Crore Noida International Airport at Jewar

Business Standard

Prime Minister Narendra Modi will inaugurate the Noida International Airport in Jewar, Uttar Pradesh today after multiple deadline misses. The first phase, built with ₹11,200 crore investment, will handle 12 million passengers annually across a 7,200-acre site with six runways planned. Zurich Airport International is the sole investor in what is planned to be India's largest airport.

Why it matters: The project tests India's ability to attract major foreign investment in infrastructure beyond domestic champions like Adani and Ambani, and will serve the Delhi-NCR region's growing aviation needs.

India Tests Local Currency Payments for Gulf Oil as Rupee Hits ₹94.1

The Hindu

India is experimenting with payments for Gulf oil in local currencies rather than US dollars as crude prices hit $123/barrel and the rupee plummets to ₹94.1 per dollar. The move aims to save 1-2% in transaction costs and reduce the fiscal impact of the double hit from rising oil prices and currency depreciation. India already pays Russia in rupees and UAE in dirhams for oil imports.

Why it matters: The shift away from dollar-denominated oil trade could accelerate de-dollarization trends and reshape global energy payment systems if other major importers follow suit.
