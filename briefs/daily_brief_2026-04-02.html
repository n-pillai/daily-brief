India's Manufacturing PMI Falls to Four-Year Low Amid Middle East Conflict

Business Standard

India's Manufacturing PMI dropped to a four-year low as output growth slowed amid softer domestic demand and uncertainty from the West Asia war. Input cost inflation hit a 43-month high in March, while new orders and output growth decelerated. The weakness comes as markets grapple with elevated oil prices and geopolitical tensions.

Why it matters: The sharp decline signals growing economic headwinds for India at a time when inflation pressures are intensifying due to the Iran conflict and rising commodity prices.

RBI Regulatory Actions Engineer Rupee Recovery Amid Dollar Pressures

Business Standard

The Reserve Bank of India's twin regulatory measures—capping banks' net open rupee positions and barring NDF offerings to corporates—forced dollar unwinding and engineered a meaningful rupee recovery. The moves, while disruptive to banking operations short-term, helped stabilize the currency amid broader market pressures from rising U.S. Treasury yields and Brent crude crossing $107 per barrel.

Why it matters: The RBI's aggressive intervention demonstrates the central bank's willingness to use regulatory tools to defend the rupee as imported inflation fears mount from elevated oil prices.

India Keeps Inflation Target Unchanged at 4% for Next Five Years

Economic Times

The Indian government reaffirmed its inflation-targeting framework, maintaining the 4% retail inflation goal with a 2-6% tolerance band for April 2026 to March 2031. The decision, announced via gazette notification by the Department of Economic Affairs in consultation with the RBI, marks the second consecutive extension of the framework first introduced in 2016 and renewed in 2021.

Why it matters: The unchanged target signals policy continuity even as India faces mounting inflationary pressures from the Middle East conflict and rising commodity costs.

Lok Sabha Passes Jan Vishwas Amendment Bill to Decriminalize 717 Provisions

Business Standard

The Lok Sabha passed the Jan Vishwas Amendment Bill proposing to amend 784 provisions across 79 Central Acts administered by 23 ministries. The legislation seeks to decriminalize 717 provisions and amend 67 provisions to facilitate ease of living and reduce the compliance burden on businesses and citizens.

Why it matters: The reforms represent a major regulatory overhaul aimed at improving India's business environment and reducing the criminalization of minor regulatory violations.

Mamata Banerjee Says EC Actions Risk Disenfranchisement of Voters in Bengal

The Hindu

West Bengal Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee accused the Election Commission of risking voter disenfranchisement through its actions ahead of the state assembly polls scheduled for April 9. The allegations come amid heightened political tensions as the TMC and BJP contest a crucial state election. Banerjee has also criticized EC transfers of top state bureaucrats and police officers.

Why it matters: The escalating confrontation between West Bengal's government and the Election Commission adds uncertainty to a high-stakes state election with national political implications.
